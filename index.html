Blog and Articles

 

Creating a webpage is both a technical craft and a form of expression. It begins with building the structure

using HTML, which serves as the skeleton that holds content, including headings, paragraphs, images, and links.

CSS then brings the design to life, adding colour, typography, layout, and visual effects to establish the overall

style and mood. JavaScript introduces interactivity, enabling buttons, animations, dynamic content, and other

features that make the site feel alive. Developing a webpage is an iterative process, much like cooking — you

gather the ingredients, follow a plan, and make adjustments until everything feels right.

 

Web design, on the other hand, is the discipline that shapes the way a webpage looks, feels, and functions for

its users.It involves applying visual design principles such as balance, contrast, and hierarchy while also focusing

on user experience to ensure the site is intuitive and engaging. It requires consideration for responsive design so

the site works seamlessly across devices, as well as accessibility to make it usable for everyone. A well-designed

site also aligns its style with the brand or purpose it serves, whether that’s playful, professional, minimalist, or bold.

In essence, building a webpage is the act of constructing the product, while web design is the art and science that

guides its creation to be both functional and visually appealing.
